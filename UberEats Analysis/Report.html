Literature Review

Joseph & Robert mentioned that red is most likely to be seen as a tense, negative and physically stimulating factor, because it’s defined as a warm color, whereas blue is the opposite, being defined as a cool color that can be perceived as calm and having positive symbolism. Sometimes the color environment has a strong influence on the outcomes of purchasing decisions therefore, it is meaningful to examine the influence of the color environment on customers' behavior in the retail environment and the possible connection between them. In the past, environmental factors were always neglected in the marketing area, because environmental factors are an often-silent language and frequently have little impact on business. Nevertheless, nowadays more companies are starting to recognize the significant and influential part of the environment’s effect on customer behavior and investigate customers’ intentions. The appropriate usage of color will not only draw the customer’s attention but can also effectively increase their purchasing intention. Despite the preference for color, Bellizzi et al claimed that a retail environment in red will stimulate customers’ buying behavior and enhance their buying consideration in physiological and psychological ways, because red is a warm color which may cause the mood to be stimulated (Bellizzi et al, 2022). However, subsequent to the experiment, the data revealed that average spending on the red-colored platform was much less than the average spending on a blue platform. In addition, the researcher discovered that there is no direct connection between the color treatment and length of shopping time. While customers spent more time in the red store, the blue store’s customer spending was far greater, because the customers in the blue store preferred to purchase higher price sets. Therefore, in a business environment, red is far more distracting than blue, but Bellizzi et al also mentioned that several factors such as age, education and income still remain to be considered.  In Experiment 2, Bellizzi et al hypothesized that the purchase intention in the red environment will be less than the blue environment due to the higher stimulation and negative evaluations that tend to enhance anxiety. On the contrary, the blue environment produces a more pleasurable feeling in customers. Therefore the customers in the blue store gave more positive feedback, with their greater desire to purchase products as they have a more pleasurable feeling while shopping

What’s more, Simms Jenkins mentioned in his book, The Truth about Email Marketing, that the proper estimated return for every dollar spent on email marketing should be $46. Email marketing delivered the most return among all internet investment strategies. Versatility is the icon of email marketing as no matter what size your company is, email marketing can always fit in the company’s needs and bring the same benefit just like others. Jenkins also mentioned that recommendation email can help you communicate with your customers and build up loyalty if used properly. A long-term relationship can be developed between the companies and their customers, which according to Jenkins is the real benefit of email marketing. Another characteristic of email marketing is audience targeting. If companies got customers’ information regarding gender, age, interests and much more personal info, email marketing can be modified to fit their habits and needs which further increase the investment return. Moreover, email is also the fastest way  to reach out customers and respond with actions immediately. According to Jenikins, email marketing’s ROI index is 70% higher than any other direct-response marketing vehicle, and over 60% of business owners said that the email is the best way for advertisers and customers to reach out. Despite the marketing email and the color of the platform, the waiting time for delivery is also an important factor for customer satisfaction. Davis and Heineke found in their study that customers are less tolerable during lunch time compared to dinner time, because the duration of lunch is limited. Davis and Heineke claim that the waiting time for customers is subjective, which is based on customer’s experience, background and value. Therefore, customers’ tolerance for waiting times is various in different situations. Usually customers recognize the waiting time as an economic and physical cost. Therefore, the customer's attitude towards waiting is important for the delivery platform’s profit and sustainable operation, and also, it could be a detrimental factor for many delivery platform companies.

Q1: Will emails with selected recommendations encourage users to use UberEats in New York city?

All of the people who have and used UberEats are the target audience. We want to randomly select people from the UberEats registered users as experimental subjects to carry out our study. We are going to randomly select 100 registered users as our subjects for this trail. Because our team wanted to find out if sending a selected recommendation email to users would increase their purchases in UberEats. Thus, we would like to split 100 subjects into two groups equally.

One group’s users will not receive any recommendation emails. It will be counted as a control group. The other group will receive selected recommendation emails from UberEats to promote their products. This group will be counted as an examined group. We set the same amount of time to do the experiment. We looked at the volume of orders placed by two groups on UberEats to determine whether selected recommendation  emails stimulated consumer spending. If people who receive the marketing email order significantly more than those who don't, then the email method is working, and vice versa. The planned duration of the experiment is 1 month, and the frequency of the ad email push is one every three days. The content will be the recommended restaurants, menu and promotion information of the UberEats’ partner merchants. We will target these 100 users on app and continue to track their orders after the experiment begins. In particular, we will observe the comparison between the two groups in terms of the number of orders placed a few days after the email push. The experiment will be conducted in a month-long cycle, where users in the experimental group will receive an email about the week's recommendations every Monday. At the end of the experiment we compare the data through the UberEats backend.The dependent variable is customers’ purchasing on Ubereats. The independent variable is whether or not users received email.

All of the data during the experiment will be collected directly by UberEats.We will train the staff involved in this trial (both our group and those on the Uber side) to ensure that all personal information of subjects is not released to the public and all data and information for this test only.

Research Question 3: “can we increase UberEats sales in New York City by providing a shorter waiting time but more delivery fee option”

For research question 3, key components of the research design are addressed below. There have been studies showing that factors, such as delivery service experience and price, would significantly influence customer intention to use food delivery apps. Price is defined as the payment that customers paid to exchange for a service or product. The delivery service experience is defined as the waiting time shown on the app/website when the user places an order online.

The population of interest is all existing or potential UberEats users. The sample selection is randomly selecting existing New York UberEats users who are active in the past 3 three months (meaning they ordered a food delivery to a New York address). The sample size would be 620. The operational procedure would be to send out questionnaires to 620 users from the treatment group and ask them to answer the question regarding an option, ‘Will you like to speed up your order with a reasonable price’. The questionnaire will state if the user selects this option, he/she would receive a 10% increase in the delivery fee and a 10% decrease in waiting time. The questionnaire will also state that the ‘10% decrease in waiting time is an approximation but we are positive that the delivery time will be decreased since we have a separate team of drivers who only do their deliveries’. It will also give respondents a real-life example, such as 'Alice lives in Queens and ordered a brunch from a diner in Manhattan. By paying only 3 dollars more in delivery fee, she saved 10 mins of her waiting time'. The Brief schedule: will be that we send out questionnaires in one month in order to make sure users from the sample group will receive the email on the same day. Then, we will give them one month to fill out the survey. For those users who failed to open the email 2 weeks after we sent out the email. We will send a reminder email to them.  Data collection: all response data will be collected, as well as each respondent’s  id, zip code, past purchase history.  Data security: we will cooperate with the UberEats marketing department because they have sufficient experience in marketing survey, email marketing and how to ensure user data security. Also, we will make sure that only people from the research team will have access to the survey data. Right now, for this question, the independent variable (treatment) is whether UberEats users would choose this service. The dependent variable(outcome) is UberEats sales ($). The other variables are response time and other responders’ information(zip code, past purchase history).
